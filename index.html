About Me

Hello ! My full name is Thế Phương Đào. I am currently a research fellow at National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI). I am working in Algorithmic Methods and Systems Biology group led by Dr. Teresa Prytycka. Before coming to NCBI, I did my graduate study in Beautiful British Columbia under Dr. Cenk Sahinalp and Dr. Martin Ester's supervision. Once upon a time, I enjoy my undergraduate study and ice skating on the Rideau Canal in Ottawa.

During grad school, I have been a research assistant at Vancouver Prostate Center. My PhD study was supported by NSERC Alexander Graham Bell Canada Graduate Scholarship (CGS-D), PIMS International Graduate Training Centre in Mathematical Biology Fellowship and Bioinformatics for Combating Infectious Disease (BCID) Project. I feel honored to work on network motif counting for my Master's thesis and obtained Erdős number 2.
